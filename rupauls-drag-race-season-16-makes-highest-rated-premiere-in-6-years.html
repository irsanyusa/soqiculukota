    RuPaul’s Drag Race season 16 has just begun, but it’s already proving to be a strong force on television. The latest season recently debuted with the best TV ratings for any season premiere in the history of the franchise.

    RuPaul’s Drag Race Season 16 Ushers In Highest-Rated Premiere

    According to live + three-day Nielsen data, the January 5 premiere of season 16 climbed up to 4% versus the previous season. Furthermore, during the three-day viewing window, Drag Race ended with the highest-rated season premiere in six years among adults 18-49.

    It pays noting, however, that this rating is close to season 15’s premiere’s rating. The premiere episode of season 16 score a 0.91. This is a 1% increase from season 15. With the 18-49 and 18-34 demographic, Drag Race was also hailed the top cable entertainment telecast of the day on Friday.

    Season 16 marks the second for Drag Race at MTV after it moved from VH1 last season. It’s a fair decision, as MTV generally performs well in terms of viewership and demo ratings than VH1.

    RuPaul’s Drag Race UK vs. the World Season Two Reveals Cast

    In another part of the world, RuPaul’s Drag Race UK vs. the World has revealed its cast for season two. All ready to strut the British Werk Room, the queens will be competing for the first-ever cash prize in UK franchise her-story.

    The 11 queens from different regions of the globe are returning to vie for the second UK vs. the World crown. They include fan-favorite contestants from international franchises.

    On behalf of the United States, RuPaul’s Drag Race queens Mayhem Miller (season 10, All Stars 5) and Scarlet Envy (season 11, All Stars 6) will attempt to snatch the crown.

    Other competitors are Arantxa Castilla-La Mancha (Drag Race España season 1); Choriza May (RuPaul’s Drag Race UK season 3); Gothy Kendoll (RuPaul’s Drag Race UK season 1); Hannah Conda (RuPaul’s Drag Race Down Under season 2); Jonbers Blonde (RuPaul’s Drag Race UK, season 4); Keta Minaj (Drag Race Holland, season 2); La Grande Dame (Drag Race France season 1); Marina Summers (Drag Race Philippines season 1), and Tia Kofi (RuPaul’s Drag Race UK season 2).

    RuPaul Charles will once again return as head judge for the series. She will be joined by Michelle Visage, Graham Norton, and Alan Carr. Lastly, to make the season more exciting, guest judges and celebrities will also make appearances throughout the season.
