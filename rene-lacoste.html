    25 Jul 2024, 00:00

    Profession: Tennis Player

    Biography: René Lacoste was a French tennis player, nicknamed "the Crocodile" for the way he dealt with opponents on the court. He is perhaps best known for creating the Lacoste tennis shirt, and for the brand which bears his name and logo today.

    Lacoste was one of four French tennis stars, known as The Four Musketeers, who dominated tennis in the 1920s and 1930s. The other three were Jean Borotra, Jacques Brugnon, and Henri Cochet.

    During his career, Lacoste won seven Grand Slam singles titles: French Open (1925, 1927, 1929), Wimbledon (1925, 1928), US Open (1926, 1927). He was world number one for the whole of 1926 and 1927.

    Born: July 2, 1904
    Birthplace: Paris, France

    Generation: Greatest Generation
    Chinese Zodiac: Dragon
    Star Sign: Cancer

    Died: October 12, 1996 (aged 92)
    Cause of Death: Heart failure

    • 1925-06-06 French Championships Men's Tennis: René Lacoste wins 1st of 3 French titles, beating fellow Frenchman Jean Borotra 7-5, 6-1, 6-4
    • 1925-07-04 Wimbledon Men's Tennis: In an all-French final René Lacoste beats Jean Borotra 6-3, 6-3, 4-6, 8-6
    • 1925-09-12 International Lawn Tennis Challenge, Philadelphia, PA: Vincent Richards & R. Norris Williams clinch US 6th straight title; beat Jean Borotra & René Lacoste of France 6-4, 6-4, 6-3 for 3-0 lead; ends 5-0
    • 1926-06-14 French Championships Men's Tennis: Henri Cochet wins 1st of 4 home titles; beats fellow Frenchman René Lacoste 6-2, 6-4, 6-3

    • 1927-06-05 French Championships Men's Tennis: Hometown favourite Rene Lacoste wins 2nd of 3 French titles; beats Bill Tilden 6-4, 4-6, 5-7, 6-3, 11-9
    • 1927-09-17 US National Championship Men's Tennis, Forest Hills NY: Frenchman Rene Lacoste successfully defends title; beats Bill Tilden 11-9, 6-3, 11-9
